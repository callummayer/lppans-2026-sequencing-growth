LPPANS 2026 · Sequencing Growth

Implementing the initial phases of a secondary plan in Fredericton's Southeast neighbourhood

zzap

The Southeast Secondary Plan is the City of Fredericton's blueprint for a fourth growth node — a complete community of 5,000–7,000 residents on roughly 200 hectares at the city's edge.

What makes it different: the City owns 65% of the plan area overall — and 100% of Phases 1 and 2. Public ownership shifts the question from "what should we allow?" to "what should we build, in what order, and on what terms?"

This work was led by the City of Fredericton's Planning & Development team, in partnership with zzap Architecture + Planning and EXP Services. Two rounds of public engagement (March–April and August–October 2025) shaped the framework.

Our session focuses on the part most plans skip: the sequencing. The framework already decides what goes where — the workshop asks how Phases 1 and 2 actually build out at a scale, mix, and orientation that adds up to a complete community.

About zzap

Architecture + Planning, working together.

zzap
From private development into the public realm

We're zzap. We design places + the plans that get them built.

Architecture and planning have lived on opposite sides of a wall for too long. We don't.

zzap is a Dartmouth-based architecture and planning practice. For most of our history we've worked with private developers — designing buildings while quietly fixing the planning problems that made them hard to build.

Southeast Fredericton is part of a deliberate move into municipal secondary planning. We bring two things most planning teams don't: built-form fluency (we've designed the things plans make possible) and delivery instinct (we know what kills a project at site-plan stage).

What we believe: a plan that can't be built is a brochure. The work isn't writing the policy — it's sequencing the moves so the policy survives contact with infrastructure, ownership, and time.

If you're a municipality with a growth area, public land, or a stalled secondary plan — we'd like to talk.
