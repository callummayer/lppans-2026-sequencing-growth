The Scenario

The City owns the land. How does that change the move?

Set the scene

The Southeast Secondary Plan is adopted. Phases 1 & 2 sit entirely on City land. The City intends to deliver the infrastructure but not develop the parcels itself — partnerships with developers will execute the plan's intent.

Your table works through how that hand-off gets structured — the disposition model, the partners, the conditions, the sequence.

What to sketch on the markup map

  1. Release sequence — mark which parcels go first, second, third. Number them.
  2. Conditions zone — circle where you'd attach the strongest disposal-agreement conditions (mix, affordability, form, frontage).
  3. Partnerships — note where you'd expect a single partner, where you'd split, where the City keeps the asset.
  4. One sentence on what triggers the move from Phase 1 to Phase 2.

Prompts to anchor the conversation

You don't have to answer all of them. They're scaffolding — if your table is circling, pick the prompt that gets it unstuck and start there. The through-line is the same: the City owns the land, intends to partner, what's the move?

  1. Disposition model Outright sale, long-term lease, joint venture, RFP-with-conditions, land trust — what disposition mechanism best delivers the plan's intent on publicly-owned land?
  2. The right partner What does the first development partner look like for Phase 1? What track record, capabilities, or commitments would you weight over price at RFP?
  3. Conditions in the sale Zoning sets envelope. What does the disposal agreement need to carry on top — mix, affordability, form, timing — so that approved zoning plus private intent actually delivers the plan?
  4. Release sequence Which parcels go first, second, third? What servicing milestone, absorption signal, or partnership state has to be true to unlock each release?
  5. The handoff Streets, parks, ground-floor commercial, affordable units — where does the City's role stop and the partner's start? Who builds, who owns, who maintains?
