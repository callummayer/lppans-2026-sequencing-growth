The Scenario

Sequencing Growth — Phases 1 & 2

Set the scene

It's 2027. The Southeast Secondary Plan is adopted. Phase 1 servicing is funded. The land-use plan already decides what goes where — mixed-use node in Phase 2, residential plus other uses in Phase 1.

Your table decides how Phases 1 and 2 actually build out at a scale that adds up to a complete community.

What to sketch on the markup map

  1. Building footprints — rough scale, where they cluster, how they front the street.
  2. Accesses — primary routes in, where active modes connect, where parking lives.
  3. The mix — mark where each land-use type lands in Phase 1 and at the Phase 2 Central Node, and roughly at what intensity.
  4. One sentence on what triggers Phase 2 — population, servicing, frontage demand, something else.

Prompts to anchor the conversation

You don't have to answer all of them. They're scaffolding — if your table is circling, pick the prompt that gets it unstuck and start there.

  1. Phase 1, day one Stand at the edge of Phase 1 on move-in morning. What does a resident need within a five-minute walk for this to feel like a neighbourhood and not a subdivision?
  2. Scale that pencils The Central Node is in Phase 2. What's the smallest viable version of it that can open without sitting empty waiting for residential to catch up?
  3. Mix without sprawl Phase 1 is residential plus "some other uses." What other uses, where, and at what frontage — without diluting the Central Node before it opens?
  4. Walkable from isolation The site has no neighbouring village. Which streets, frontages, or block sizes in Phases 1 and 2 do the most work to discourage car-only living before the rest of the node arrives?
  5. The trigger What has to be true on the ground for it to be the right moment to move from Phase 1 into Phase 2? Population? Absorption? Servicing milestone? Something else?
