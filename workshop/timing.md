# Workshop Runsheet — 30 minutes

**Session:** Sequencing Growth — LPPANS 2026
**Date:** May 15, 2026 · 10:00–10:45 AM
**Room:** Lord Nelson Hotel, Halifax

---

## 0:00 — End of Connor's 12-min talk

Hand off straight from the workshop-intro slide into the prompts slide. The prompts slide stays on screen for the whole workshop. No applause break, no transition. Keep momentum.

## 0:00–0:05 · Set the table (5 min)

**Connor at the front:**

- Restate the scenario in one sentence: *"It's 2027. The secondary plan is adopted. Phase 1 servicing is funded. The land-use plan tells you what goes roughly where — your table figures out how Phases 1 and 2 actually build out at a scale that adds up to a complete community."*
- Be explicit about what the workshop is **not**: *"This is not 'where should the mixed-use node go.' The plan already decides that — it's in Phase 2, Phase 1 is residential plus other uses. You're working inside the framework, not redoing it."*
- Walk through what's on each table:
  - **Two catalog sets** of the secondary-plan schedules: road network, open-space network, land use plan, demonstration plan (reference material)
  - **One large blown-up Phases 1 & 2 map** — the markup sheet. *This is what they draw on.*
  - **Prompt card** (double-sided, same five prompts as the screen)
- **QR code on screen** for a digital copy if anyone wants to mark up on an iPad.
- Tell tables what to record on the markup sheet: *sketch buildings, accesses, frontages, scales, orientations. Mark where each land-use type lands and at what intensity. Write one sentence on what triggers Phase 2.*
- Name a reporter at each table (whoever is sitting closest to the door — keeps it fast).
- Sarah and Connor will float.

**Watch for:** tables waiting for permission. Once you've said "go," walk away from the front so they start.

## 0:05–0:15 · Round-table discussion (10 min)

**Connor + Sarah floating, splitting the room.**

This is talk-first, draw-later. Tables are reading the schedules, debating mix and scale, surfacing assumptions about what makes a complete community work on a greenfield site.

Job while floating:

- **First 5 minutes:** every table at least once. Look for tables that haven't started — ask: *"What mix of uses do you want a Phase 1 resident to be able to walk to on day one?"*
- **Last 5 minutes:** redirect tables drifting into "where should the mixed-use node be" — *"The plan put the node in Phase 2. Given that, what does Phase 1 need to do for the node to succeed when it opens?"*

**Don't:** give them the answer. The exercise is about the *thinking*, not arriving at the plan's solution.

## 0:15–0:25 · Mark it up (10 min)

**Tables move from talking to drawing.** Sketch directly on the Phases 1 & 2 map: building footprints, accesses, frontages, where the mix concentrates, where the corridor activates.

Job while floating:

- Look for tables that haven't picked up a marker. Ask the quiet person directly: *"What would you draw first?"*
- Tables drawing a perfect plan: redirect to scale. *"At what density? How many units in Phase 1?"*
- Last 3 minutes: call time. *"Three minutes. Pick the two decisions you most want to defend."*

## 0:25–0:30 · Report-back (5 min)

**Connor at the front, mic to the reporters.**

- 30–45 seconds per table — strict. Hold up a hand at 30 seconds.
- One question to each reporter: *"What's the one move in your Phase 1 that makes Phase 2 work? And what was the hardest trade-off?"*
- Don't editorialize between tables. Just collect. Take photos.

If you have 6+ tables and time is tight: ask 3 reporters at random, then offer the rest: *"Anything genuinely different from what we've heard?"*

If you have a minute left at the end: land the closer — *"What's hard here is what's hard about every secondary plan once it's adopted. The framework decides where. Sequencing decides whether it works."*

If you ran over, drop the closer and just thank the room.

- Spare prompt cards (5)

## If something breaks

- **Projector dies:** Connor talks through the deck from memory + leave-behind. Hand out prompt cards immediately so the prompts are still on the table. Workshop runs unchanged.
- **Audience is small (<15):** collapse to two tables, do the activity at one combined table, extend report-back to 8 min.
- **Audience is large (>40):** add a "two minutes to compare with the next table" beat between table-work and report-back. Cuts report-back to 3 min total but keeps energy high.
