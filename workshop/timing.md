# Workshop Runsheet — 25 minutes

**Session:** Sequencing Growth — LPPANS 2026
**Date:** May 15, 2026 · 10:00–10:45 AM
**Room:** Lord Nelson Hotel, Halifax

Total session is 45 min: ~14 min talk + 25 min workshop + buffer.

---

## 0:00 — End of Connor's ~14-min talk

Hand off straight from the workshop-intro slide. After workshop-intro Connor lands briefly on the zones-overview slide (~30 sec — point at the three tags, name the job each zone does, no parameters), then to the closing slide and finally the prompts slide. **The prompts slide stays on screen for the whole workshop.** No applause break, no transition. Keep momentum. The full schedule for each zone lives on the **zones handout** at each table — that's where tables go for detail.

## 0:00–0:05 · Set the table (5 min)

**Connor at the front:**

- Restate the scenario in one sentence: *"The plan is approved. Phases 1 and 2 sit entirely on City land. The City wants to deliver the infrastructure but partner out the parcels. Your table works through how that hand-off gets structured."*
- Be explicit about what the workshop is **not**: *"This is not 'redraw the land-use plan.' Zoning is set. You're working on what the disposal agreements need to do — model, partner, conditions, sequence, handoff."*
- Walk through what's on each table:
  - **Two catalog sets** of the secondary-plan schedules: road network, open-space network, land use plan, demonstration plan (reference material)
  - **One large blown-up Phases 1 & 2 map** with trace paper laid over it — the markup sheet. *This is what they draw on.*
  - **Prompt card** (double-sided, same five prompts as the screen)
  - **Zones handout** (single 11×17 landscape sheet — MR-5, MX-3, R-5 with intent, permits, parameters) — sits next to the markup map for quick lookup as the table sketches
- **QR code on screen** for a digital copy of every schedule, including the secondary plan in full, if anyone wants to dig deeper.
- Tell tables what to record on the markup sheet: *number the release sequence, circle conditions zones, note partner types, and write one sentence on what triggers the move from Phase 1 to Phase 2.*
- Name a reporter at each table (whoever is sitting closest to the door — keeps it fast).
- Sarah and Connor will float.

**Watch for:** tables waiting for permission. Once you've said "go," walk away from the front so they start.

## 0:05–0:20 · Round-table discussion + markup (15 min)

**Connor + Sarah floating, splitting the room.**

This is the merged discussion + drawing block. Tables talk through the disposition prompts and illustrate decisions on the trace paper as they go. No artificial separation between talk and draw — moves overlap with conversation.

Job while floating:

- **First 5 minutes:** every table at least once. Look for tables that haven't started — ask: *"What's the first parcel the City releases? Why that one?"*
- **Middle 5 minutes:** push tables that are still talking to start sketching. *"Mark the release sequence on the map. Number them."*
- **Last 5 minutes:** redirect tables drifting into "redesign the plan" — *"Zoning is set. What does the disposal agreement carry on top so it actually delivers what's intended?"*
- Last 2 minutes: call time. *"Two minutes. Pick the two decisions you most want to defend."*

**Don't:** give them the answer. The exercise is about the *thinking*, not arriving at the plan's solution.

## 0:20–0:25 · Report-back (5 min)

**Connor at the front, mic to the reporters.**

- Two tables only. Pick the two whose markups looked most distinct from each other while floating. 60–90 seconds each.
- One question per reporter: *"What's the one condition in your disposal agreement you'd fight hardest to keep? And what was the hardest trade-off?"*
- Don't editorialize between tables. Just collect. Take photos.

If you have time left at the end: land the closer — *"Zoning sets envelope. Land disposition is what gets that envelope built — or not, or built wrong. On publicly-owned land, the disposal agreement is the planning instrument that matters most after adoption."*

If you ran over, drop the closer and just thank the room.

## If something breaks

- **Projector dies:** Connor talks through the deck from memory + leave-behind. Hand out prompt cards immediately so the prompts are still on the table. Workshop runs unchanged.
- **Audience is small (<15):** collapse to two tables, do the activity at one combined table, extend report-back to 8 min.
- **Audience is large (>40):** add a "two minutes to compare with the next table" beat between table-work and report-back. Cuts report-back to 3 min total but keeps energy high.
