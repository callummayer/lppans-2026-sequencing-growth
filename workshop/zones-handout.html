Reference · Zones applied in Phases 1 & 2 / zzap

Three zones. Three jobs.

The Southeast Secondary Plan applies three zones across Phases 1 & 2. Keep this sheet next to the markup map when you're testing a sequencing decision against what each zone actually permits.

MR-5

Multi-Residential Zone Five

Multi-residential infill in the form of apartment buildings, with allowance for limited convenience commercial on the ground floor. Located along public transit corridors. Provides for high-rise building height. More than one main residential building permitted on a lot.

What it permits

  • By rightApartment building · assisted living.
  • ConditionalChild-care centre (large) · convenience store · food-service take-out · personal service (apparel & appearance) · single-room occupancy.

General parameters

Density (max)160 u/ha · bonus to 333 u/ha for affordable
Lot coverage (max)45 % of lot area
Building height (max)24 m (higher with Mid-Town overlay)
Lot frontage (min)30 m
Landscaped area (min)35 % at grade

Convenience commercial limited to 90 m² per unit, max 15 % of ground floor, ground floor only.

City of Fredericton Zoning By-law §9.6 (MR-5)

MX-3

Mixed Use Zone Three

Larger-scale buildings with commercial uses on lower floors and a required residential component above. Along arterials or collectors. More than one main building permitted on a lot. This is the zone applied to the Central Node.

What it permits

  • By right — commercialRetail · grocery · restaurant · café / take-out · office · financial institution · fitness centre · medical practice · cultural establishment · studios (artisan, media, photo) · social organization.
  • By right — residentialApartment · dwelling unit · stacked townhouse · townhouse · workforce housing · assisted living.
  • ConditionalMedical clinic · child-care centre (large) · single-room occupancy.

General parameters

Density (max)100 u/ha · up to 285 in Mid-Town · 333 affordable
Lot coverage (max)60 % of lot area
Building height9 m min — 24 m max
Residential (min)25 % of GFA must be dwelling units
Floor caps (per use)Retail 300 m² · grocery 700 m² · office 500 m²

Residential cannot occupy ground-floor frontage and must have a separate entrance from any commercial use.

City of Fredericton Zoning By-law §11.13 (MX-3)

R-5

Residential Zone Five

Low-rise residential with a comprehensive mix of housing forms — single detached, duplex, semi-detached, townhouse, cluster. Designed for suburban greenfield contexts on large parcels. Up to three secondary dwelling units permitted per lot.

What it permits

  • By rightSingle detached · semi-detached · duplex · townhouse · secondary dwelling units (up to 3 / lot) · home occupation · child-care centre (small) · group home (limited) · keeping of hens.
  • ConditionalCluster housing · child-care centre (medium) · group home · tourist home · single-room occupancy.

General parameters

Mix requirementSingle detached capped at 80 % of total units
Single-detached standardsFollow R-1 (§8.2(4))
Duplex / semi-detachedFollow R-3 (§8.3(4))
Townhouse / clusterFollow MR-1 (§9.2(4))
Secondary unitsUp to 3 per lot

The 80 % single-detached cap is the lever — it forces mix at the parcel level, not just the plan-area average.

City of Fredericton Zoning By-law §8.5 (R-5)
